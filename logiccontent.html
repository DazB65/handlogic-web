How LogicContent works

Ship a feature. Get a week of content.

Built on how indie apps actually get discovered — not another social media dashboard.

1

Describe your apps once

What each app does, who it's for, and how you talk. That context powers everything it writes — in your voice, not a marketer's.

2

Fan out your changelogs

Paste a feature or a rough idea, and get ready-to-post content for X, Facebook, YouTube and Reddit in one click — plus launch kits for Product Hunt, Hacker News, and press pitches.

3

Do the week, log the results

A queue of 3–5 tasks, each under 30 minutes, with a streak to keep you honest. Everything you post gets logged so you learn which channel actually works.

Features

The system, not the willpower.

For developers who can ship code every week but fall off the wagon when it's time to tell anyone.

📅

This Week queue

3–5 concrete marketing tasks a week, each under 30 minutes, planned around your apps and ideas. A streak counter keeps the habit alive.

🪄

Content fan-out

One changelog entry becomes an X thread, a Facebook post, a YouTube script, and a sincere Reddit post — each written for its platform, ready to copy.

🚀

Launch kits

Product Hunt maker comments, Show HN posts, directory checklists, and press pitches to Mac blogs — generated per app, reusable every release.

💡

Idea bank

Capture content ideas the moment they occur, before they vanish. One click sends any idea into the fan-out.

📊

Results log

Every post gets logged with its results, per channel — so after a month you know where your users actually come from.

🔑

Your key, your data

Bring your own OpenAI API key. Everything stays local on your Mac — no accounts, no cloud, no telemetry.
